Context

Avant Gardist brings the fashion brave together with rising designers.

Imagine getting access to quirky independent fashion designers from all around the world in a one-stop-shop.

Avant Gardist is a global fashion community startup. They are on a mission to connect emerging designers from all over the world with adventurous fashion-conscious shoppers.

As part of my SVC Capstone, I and three other UX designers worked with the owner of the startup concept shop Avant Gardist. Our goal was to design a personalized shopping experience for customers.

For this project, I took lead on the information architecture, visual, and interaction design and I was the main prototyper for the mobile designs, I implemented our concepts from low to high-fidelity based on instructor's, peers', and client's feedback and I helped my teammates with research. By the end of the project, we delivered a design for a responsive end-to-end website.

Problem

Avant Gardist is a seed-stage startup that lacks an online presence. 

Avant Gardist is a new startup that aims to sell clothes, shoes, and accessories from unknown fashion designers to adventurous shoppers. However, Avant Gardist doesn‚Äôt have a reputation in the fashion world yet.

We wanted to create a personalized shopping experience that is on par with or better than more established competitor sites.

Design Opportunity

Design a recommendation experience that will serve up personalized suggestions to users based on their preferences.

Business Goals

Increase the number of customers and retention rate by establishing trust with new users, resulting in increasing sales profit.

Understanding the user

Premium customers who shop from boutiques and are interested in unique (bold) pieces.

Research

In our initial client brief, Michelle from Avant Gardist said that she wanted to create a personalized experience for customers to shop in an international marketplace from the comfort of their own homes. 

Our first step was to conduct a competitive analysis to help us understand the market, common designs, and overall user experiences.

Then we surveyed fashion enthusiasts and interviewed two international fashion designers to help us understand the fashion industry.

Competitive Analysis

I started by comparing different websites to help us understand their user flow starting with the signup flow to the checkout.

Interviews

We were able to interview two fashion designers on an international level in order to understand the following:

  • How potential customers on the Avant Gardist website shop for fashion and what would appeal to them about buying from emerging designers

  • The interaction between a shopping website and social media usage

  • Find out what features of a site like Avant Gardist would be useful


    I interviewed Reema Dahbour and she helped us understand the fashion world more, I learned that our users aren't the same ones who would shop at Gucci. We were also advised to use the term "Genderless" in our designs rather than "Unisex" and that's what Avant Gardist initially had. Based on some of the insights we gained, we were able to form some of the "style quiz" questions we wanted to include in our designs.

Surveys

All four of us worked together to put up the survey questions and we were able to send it out to 32 participants. Based on this research we compiled a list of needs expressed by online shoppers:

Solution

Based on our research insights we decided to go mobile-first approach and we were able to create a personalized user experience. I started by sketching some of the screens we wanted to create.

Style Guide for Avant Gardist

UI Design

Style Guide

I took the lead in creating a style guide based on the client's brand identity in order to unify the design language across mobile and desktop.

Reusable Figma Components

Sign Up / Sign In

After analyzing different competitor's websites' sign-up flows, we decided to go with a straightforward short registration form, in order to encourage more users to register so they get the full "personalized recommendation experience" that Avant Gardist offers.

Style Quiz

After users created a basic profile, they would be directed to a short fashion quiz that would allow Avant Gardist to learn what they liked to wear, then provide recommendations based on that information. 

The style quiz screens were created by one of my teammates.

Home Page

We wanted to give shoppers a sense of exclusivity and premium by recommending designers and fashion pieces according to their personal style.

Guest user: The page won't be customized, but to increase the sign-ups, I added a section that invites customers to sign up and create a profile, by sharing the value of what they'll get in return once they sign up; designers and fashion pieces recommendations according to their taste based on the style quiz they'll do.

Logged in: For the logged-in experience; the user will be welcomed by their name and the experience will be more customized to them. I had the idea of personalizing the selection of designers and fashion pieces for the logged-in user. I've also added items recently viewed so users can continue their shopping journey where they left off.

Category Page

The user can sort and filter items, they can also add any product to their favorite.

According to our research, our potential users rely so much on social media in getting their fashion inspiration, and our client wanted a similar experience to Instagram where users can follow designers to stay up to date on their latest work. So we implemented that in our designs, and included a gallery for the designer along with their story and who they are.

Conclusion

Avant Gardist is a new business that lacks a significant online presence. This gave us the opportunity to create a delightful onboarding experience.

By the end of the 10-week capstone project, we delivered research, a competitive analysis, a style guide, and high-fidelity mockups.

Our client, Michelle, was impressed and happy with what we delivered and took our designs for further implementation and even offered if any of us wanted to continue working with her on the project.

I continued helping Michelle for a bit in defining her designs, photography, and branding strategy in terms of social media marketing (Instagram content creation). Being a fashion enthusiast myself and being close friends with Reema Dahbour helped me provide Michelle with the latest fashion trends in the industry and how to position herself as a rising online fashion platform.

This project taught me a lot about project management and how to prioritize tasks, especially in a remote environment due to Covid-19.
