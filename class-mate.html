Introduction

Introduction

ClassMate educational platform desktop application is a tool designed to facilitate collaboration and communication between university students. Its purpose is to provide a centralized location for students to connect with each other, stay up-to-date on class events and assignments, and manage their academic workload.

The application features a variety of tools, including a dashboard, calendar, note-taking feature, messaging system, to-do list, and a classmate section. These tools are designed to streamline the student experience and promote collaboration and productivity.

Problem

Problem

The problem that the ClassMate educational platform desktop application addresses is the lack of an effective tool for university students to connect and collaborate with each other. Traditionally, students have relied on in-person interactions, email, and other disjointed methods to communicate and stay organized. This often leads to missed messages, confusion, and disorganization.

Furthermore, many students struggle to keep up with their academic workload and manage their time effectively. They may have trouble remembering important due dates or staying on top of assignments, leading to stress and a decrease in academic performance.

Solution

The Classmate educational platform desktop application seeks to solve these problems by providing students with a comprehensive set of tools to communicate, organize, and collaborate effectively. By streamlining the student experience and promoting collaboration, the application aims to improve academic performance and reduce stress for students.

Goal

The goal of the ClassMate educational platform desktop application is to create a centralized hub for university students to connect, collaborate, and stay organized. The application aims to promote a sense of community and facilitate communication between students, ultimately leading to improved academic performance and success.

The Goals include:

  • Facilitating communication between students and the department president or faculty.

  • Helping students stay organized and manage their academic workload more effectively.

  • Providing a platform for students to connect and collaborate with each other.

  • Promoting productivity and reducing procrastination by providing a to-do list feature.

  • Enhancing the student experience and promoting a sense of community within the university

  • Providing a platform for students to connect and collaborate with each other.

Target Audience

The target audience for the ClassMate educational platform desktop application is university students, specifically those who are seeking a way to connect and collaborate with their classmates on assignments, events, and other academic-related activities. The application is designed to be accessible to students from various academic backgrounds and fields of study, and is intended to be used by students of all skill levels, from beginners to advanced users. The application is tailored to meet the needs of modern university students who are looking for a comprehensive solution that simplifies the way they manage their academic lives.

Competitive Advantage

The competitive advantage of the ClassMate educational platform desktop application is its ability to provide a centralized hub for students to collaborate and communicate on academic matters. The application offers features such as the ability to create and manage events, set reminders and to-do lists, and chat with classmates. This allows for a more efficient and effective learning experience as students can easily stay up-to-date with their academic schedules, assignments, and communicate with each other. The platform's user-friendly interface and focus on collaboration set it apart from other educational applications, making it a valuable tool for students to stay organized and connected.

User Journey Map

Persona: Wale-Durojaye

  • Discovery: Wale-Durojaye is added late to the ClassMate educational platform by department president.

  • Sign up: Wale-Durojaye signs up for ClassMate using his university email and creates a password. He sets up his profile and adds a profile picture.

  • Dashboard: Wale-Durojaye logs into ClassMate and sees the dashboard with conversations, who is active and who mentioned him in a conversation. He sees a conversation about an upcoming assignment and decides to join the discussion.

  • Chats: Wale-Durojaye joins the conversation about the assignment and asks for clarification on the requirements. He also shares his thoughts and asks for feedback on his ideas.

  • Calendar: Wale-Durojaye checks the calendar for upcoming events and sees that there is a Seminar coming up. He clicks on the event to see the details and adds it to his schedule.

  • To-do list: Wale-Durojaye opens his to-do list and adds tasks related to the upcoming assignment and the Seminar. He sets deadlines and priorities for each task.

  • Notes: Wale-Durojaye opens the notes section and takes down important points from the conversation about the assignment. He also takes notes on the hackathon event and the skills he wants to develop.

  • Classmates: Wale-Durojaye clicks on the classmates section and sees a list of his classmates. He clicks on a classmate's profile and sends a message to introduce himself and ask for help with the upcoming assignment.

  • Notifications: Wale-Durojaye sees a notification that his assignment submission is due in two days. He clicks on the notification and goes to the assignment page to check the requirements and submit his work.
